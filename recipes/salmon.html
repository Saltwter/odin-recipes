Salmon

Description

A lot of recipes call for grilled salmon, but honestly I prefer baking salmon in the oven - the flavors are way better! And the outside isn't chewy. A lot of grilled salmon is overcooked, resulting in a chewy texture.

This recipe calls for wild salmon. You can use farmed salmon, but trust me - wild is way better. You can also substitute uzu juice for lemon juice as it can be difficult to get uzu in season. The uzu flavor is more pronounced, but lemon works just fine.

Ingredients

  • Wild salmon - 1 lb
  • Asparagus - 1/2 bundle
  • Buttter - 3 Tbsp
  • Uzu juice (or lemon juice) - 1 Tbsp
  • Lemon zest - 1/2 lemon
  • Garlic - 2 cloves (1 minced / 1 grated)
  • Red pepper flakes - 1 tsp
  • Kosher salt - 1/2 tsp + 1/8 tsp to taste
  • Fresh ground pepper - 1/4 tsp
  • Fresh dill - use sparingly
  • Olive oil (to coat pan)
  • Rice-a-Roni - 1 box

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees.
  2. Unpack salmon and remove any bones if any remain.
  3. Tear off a sheet of aluminum foil and place over a baking sheet. Apply a small amount of olive oil to coat surface. Place salmon on center of baking sheet.
  4. Mince and grate garlic.
  5. In a small bowl mix salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes.
  6. Grate zest of half a lemon and set aside.
  7. Prepare dill - rinse and set aside.
  8. Prepare asparagus - rinse and cut off ends. Place in baking sheet surrounding salmon.
  9. In a small saucepan add butter and melt at low heat. Add garlic and spices. Cook for one minute, stirring occassionally. Remove from heat. Add uzu or lemon juice to saucepan. Stir.
  10. Pour mixture over salmon and use a brush to coat all surfaces of salmon and asparagus.
  11. Sprinkle lemon zest on salmon. Add dill to salmon.
  12. Place baking sheet in oven. Cook until internal temperature reaches 125 degrees (approximately 20 minutes)
  13. Cook Rice-a-Roni (or rice pilaf) on stovetop
  14. Enjoy!
